Tips for good photos

  • Daylight or bright white LED — avoid yellow indoor bulbs (skews readings).
  • Plain white or gray background behind clear glass — color reads correctly.
  • Don't use flash — creates highlights that confuse the matcher.
  • Fill most of the frame with the solution. Tap the middle of the clearest area.
  • Camera matches are approximate — always cross-check with reference colors and chemistry context.

Set up Ask Claude

Have a conversation grounded in your actual knowledge base — README, protocols, color guide. Claude reads them and cites specific docs in its answers, so it can't drift into generic chemistry hallucinations.
